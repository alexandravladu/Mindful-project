Sadness

What is it?

Sadness is an emotion typically characterised by a plethora of accompanying feelings, like loss, disappointment, disinterest, a low mood, helplessness and hopelessness. Though it is causes vary, the symptoms of sadness are generally the same from one individual to another, and include a low mood, lethargy, social withdrawal, periods of crying, and distractedness.

Why do you feel it?

Sadness also serves a personal function. It moves one’s attention and focus towards the self, motivating them to evaluate their priorities. This is related to the motivational functional of sadness mentioned above. An evaluation of one’s wants and goals often follows sadness, as there is a tendency to be aversive to the emotion. It can signal that something is not right, that a current circumstance or situation is not serving one's interests, and that one's behaviours and motivations should be re-assessed.

What can you do to feel better?

✪ Take a deep breath. Breathing deeply and slowly triggers the body to stop releasing stress hormones and start to relax.

✪ Try writing in order to find the root of sadness.

✪ Practice gratitude, listen to relaxing music.

✪ Go for a walk, eat your favorite food.

✪ Talk to someone you trust and/or a therapist!
